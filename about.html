Albert Renger-Patzsch
Essen, 1929



A future historical brief will report on the present age as our biologists recount the anoxic zones of the proterozoic. Technical rationales that re-present (e.g. the forest as timber supply, river as hydroelectric source, earth as a mineral deposit) satisfy untold desires and comforts. Yet they obfuscate things as things disclose themselves. Overseen by corporate sovereigns, this state, of things stripped of immanence, regulates the possible outcomes of thought and social experience toward its self-consolidation. With truth of the object lost in a beyond, life is metaphysically transfixed, in a word: inert.

Yet if breathing is still possible, the object remains present in perfect entirety, an infinite wealth dispersed in space and time, for which no bounds can be found. Entering this wealth by division, the task surfaces of becoming ourselves arbiter of the periphery of nature and construction, revealing the former in its essence and the latter in its full potency. A zone configured as such necessarily obstructs the reified feudal re-presentation. What surprises then is not the latest technical achievement, but that the depths of what was here all along swallow whole domains of science, threaten the edifice of history entirely. The age concludes, the brief notes, realizing —

All things that are yonder are also here.
(Plotinus, Enneads)
