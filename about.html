Albert Renger-Patzsch
Essen, 1929



A future historical brief reports on the present age as our biologists recount the anoxic zones of the proterozoic. Technical rationales that re-present [e.g. the forest as timber supply, river as hydroelectric source, earth as a mineral deposit] satisfy untold desires and comforts. Yet they obscure the object as it discloses itself [contemporaneous sources label this a "crisis of meaning"]. Stripped of immanence, the terrain of experience forms the inert support of a transfixed world, overseen by corporate sovereigns, that regulates the possible outcomes of thought and social experience toward self-consolidation. Truth of the object assigned to a beyond, life attends to a microbial scouring of being for its duration...

Yet if breathing is still possible, the object remains present in perfect entirety, an infinite wealth dispersed in space and time, for which no bounds can be found. Entering into this wealth by division, the intrepid soul finds itself lost in traces of an order that overshadows its feudal tasks. Here the dawn tides of ironated oceans are found as rich in content as the macabre customs still practiced on the periphery of the regime. As if in a nuclear exclusion zone, there emerges the task of becoming ourselves arbiter of the value of nature and construction, a revealing that necessarily obstructs the monolithic schema of technical re-presentation. What surprises then is not the latest scientific achievement, but that the upwelling of what was here all along subducts whole domains of science, threatens the edifice of history entirely. The age concludes, observes the brief, realizing —

Everything that is yonder is also here.
(Plotinus, Enneads)
